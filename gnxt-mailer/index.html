Why AI Work Is Uniquely Suited to Distributed Teams

AI engineering in 2026 is deep technical work — agent orchestration, MCP integration, evaluation design, production observability. These skills exist in a talent pool so small and so globally distributed that restricting your search to commuting distance of your HQ is like fishing in a puddle when the ocean is right there.

The most productive AI engineers don't need to be in a room with you. They need clear objectives, the right tools, and uninterrupted focus. And they overwhelmingly choose remote — which means your in-office requirement is a filter that removes your strongest candidates before they even apply.

AI projects are also phase-based, not continuous. A 90-day agentic sprint needs an AI architect and an MCP engineer. Deployment needs an MLOps specialist. A compliance sprint needs a governance engineer. Requiring all of these to be permanent, colocated employees is an artifact of 20th-century thinking — not how modern AI delivery works.
